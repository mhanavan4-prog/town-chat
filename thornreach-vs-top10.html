The market in three lines

The genre is contracting at the middle and thriving at the poles. Mid-tier gacha RPGs are dying — Tarisland (Nov 2025), Dragonheir's US service, and Krafton's Abyss of Dungeons (Jan 2026) all shut down, and Netflix delisted 20+ premium mobile games including Hades. The two poles that grew: fairness-first community games (OSRS's record year) and tight hybridcasual dungeon loops (Archero 2 at ~$27M/month, Habby past $2B lifetime).

Two more currents worth knowing: every major RPG added a roguelike dungeon mode in 2024–26 (Simulated Universe, Arcane Labyrinth, Grim Forest, Fractured Plane, DnF's CN roguelike), and Korea's MMO wave went cross-platform by default (Aion 2, Odin) — the direction Thornreach's one-shared-world design already points.

🕯️ Thornreach, on paper

  • What it is: a cozy witchy 3D multiplayer town where every building is a chatroom — with 5 classes (12–15 abilities each), skill trees, per-class six-chapter campaigns, 18+ side quests, night mobs, the Wilds, Ember Wastes, and 4 level-gated dungeon tiers.
  • Model: free everywhere (instant web join); $2.99/24h Town Pass for two leisure venues; Moonstones ($1.99–$9.99) buy weekly-rotating legendaries that are stat-capped at relic parity — prestige, never power. No gacha, no energy, no ads.
  • Platforms: one shared world across web, iOS, Android, and desktop.
  • Scale: solo-dev, pre-launch on stores, friends-scale backend (in-memory + JSON persistence).

Cramped skill layouts are the top control complaint. Official gamepad support since launch; KR has a Windows client with full cross-progression. KR/CN only, no English.

🎮 Gameplay & content

No classes, no mandated path: 24 skills (Sailing, the first new skill in OSRS history, shipped Nov 2025 after a community vote), tick-based combat with real skill expression, and the genre's gold-standard quests — 180 bespoke scripted adventures with puzzles and humor, zero kill-x filler, gating major unlocks. Endgame spans raids (2–8 player, a fourth arriving late 2026), inferno-tier challenges, and a collection log that outlasts 10,000 hours. One seamless persistent world shared with PC.

💰 Monetization

One subscription ($14.99/month after the March 2026 increase; ~$11/month annual), tradeable Bonds that let rich players play free with in-game gold, a permanent free tier — and nothing else. No gacha, no lootboxes, no pay-for-power, ever. The model is the genre's fairness benchmark; the trust wobble is pricing itself (two hikes in two years, plus the Jan 2025 survey floating $33 tiers and ads that triggered mass cancellations and a CEO apology).

🔁 Retention

Horizontal and self-directed: skill grinds, achievement diaries, combat achievements, pets, the quest cape — no energy systems, no daily chores, burnout the only gate. Leagues (temporary region-locked rulesets with wild relic powers) reliably resurrect lapsed players every year without adding permanent content; Group Ironman gives friend-groups a shared fresh-start economy; 500-member clans and the Grand Exchange plaza carry a 20-year chat culture.

📱 Mobile UX

The whole uncompromised MMO in a 38MB download with true PC cross-play — and the list's weakest touch experience: long-press context menus, misclicks, typing-heavy chat, and a Tutorial-Island-then-figure-it-out onboarding that quietly assumes the wiki. Jagex's 2026 summit prioritized mobile stability/performance work.

📜Playbook for Thornreach

What the ten reviews add up to — organized as what to protect, what to steal, and what to fix.

🛡️ Protect — the moats

  • The social fabric. Chat-first design exists nowhere in the top 10; only DnF's town channels and OSRS's Grand Exchange come close — and both games are enormous partly because of it. Everyone else outsources community to Discord. Keep the town the point.
  • The fairness charter. No gacha, no energy, stat-capped legendaries, $9.99 ceiling. RAID's 2.3/10 Metacritic and Diablo Immortal's unhealed launch wound show the resentment is real and permanent; OSRS's record year shows fairness compounds. Put "no gacha, no odds, nothing pay-to-win — ever" in the store description.
  • The zero-friction front door. A URL that drops you into the shared world beats 3.6–52GB installs. None of the ten can do it. Every screenshot, ad, or tweet can end in "play in 10 seconds."
  • One world, every platform. Web + phone + desktop in the same town is where the industry is heading (Korea's cross-platform wave, Steam clients everywhere). You're already there.

🗝️ Steal — proven patterns, your tone

  • A roguelike delve mode (HSR's Simulated Universe, AFK's Arcane Labyrinth, Archero 2's whole loop, DnF's new CN mode — four independent proofs). Weekly-seeded modifier runs on your existing four dungeon tiers, with a pick-1-of-3 boon draft: the cheapest infinite content in the genre.
  • Quick-delve session shape (Archero 2): a 3–5 minute dungeon run that starts in two taps. Your night cycle is the mood; this is the bus-stop version.
  • Named dungeons (Solo Leveling's gates): give T1–T4 names, signature bosses, and lore plaques. Dungeons sell the genre — put them on the store screenshots.
  • Guilt-free returns (AFK Journey): bank offline harvest regrowth and cooled-down quests into a "while you were gone" letter. Lapsed players should land on a gift, not a guilt trip.
  • Covens (Diablo's Warbands): 8-player persistent groups with a shared bank tab and a claimable table in a tavern. Small, named, home-based — not anonymous 500-member clans.
  • A Leagues-style event (OSRS): a month-long "Blood Moon" town with a twisted ruleset and cosmetic trophies. Retention without permanent content.
  • Co-op boss hunts (Summoners War's 3-player rifts, Genshin's 4-player domains): party-scaled dungeon bosses make your parties matter.
  • A calendar players can feel (RAID, minus the menace): Peddler Monday is one beat; add a weekend hunt tournament and a monthly festival. Three beats is a rhythm.
  • Onboarding compression (Solo Leveling post-EVOLUTION): a visible win — first ability, first kill, first gold — inside the first 20 minutes, with the Journal naming the next goal.
  • A 30-day resident pass (Genshin's Welkin, HSR's Express Pass — the single best-liked IAP shape in mobile): a $4.99/30-day Town Pass alongside the $2.99 day pass. Same venues-only scope, subscription-shaped value.

🔧 Fix — the honest gaps

  • Content volume. ~2 hours × 5 campaigns vs 30–1,000 hours. Don't out-author the giants — out-rotate them with the systems above (delves, events, seasons of modifiers).
  • Retention plumbing. No push notifications, login streaks, leaderboards, or social groups yet. The nightly first-kill trophy and hunt streaks are good instincts — give them a board in the town square and a phone notification when the moon rises.
  • Backend durability. JSON files on an ephemeral host is friends-scale; a store launch needs a real database, or the first viral night erases a town. (The auction sweep and account system deserve to survive a redeploy.)
  • Discoverability. "Cozy witchy MMO-lite" keywords are good; dungeon-named screenshots, a 15-second "URL to playing" clip, and the fairness pledge are the store-page story the top 10 can't tell.
  • Prove the touch UX at scale. The rebuilt thumb layout and one-panel rule are genuinely competitive; controller support (standard in DI/Genshin/SLA) is the next expectation for the desktop build.
"The cozy witchy town where the dungeon crawl is something you do with people — not instead of them." — the one sentence none of the top ten can say.

Market context for courage: the mobile RPG middle is collapsing (Tarisland, Dragonheir, Abyss of Dungeons all shut down in the last year), while the two poles — fairness-first community games and tight dungeon-run loops — both grew. Thornreach's lane sits between those poles and is currently unoccupied in the West.
